Note: The video does not have sound. 😊

Hey guys, I’ve been using this really cool web app recently and I wanted to show it off to you, since I figured ya’ll would really like it too! What it is, is a port for the classic audio player Winamp. But here’s the thing: you don’t have to download anything. It works in your browser. And it’s fully functioning!

As some of you know, Winamp is still capable of running as a standard program if you know where to find it, but this is something that often glitches and breaks in newer versions of Windows, and it looks like we aren’t getting any new updates for it any time soon. Not only that, but it’s not native to other operating systems, such as MacOS, ChromeOS, or Linux. 

But today, that all changes with WebAmp!

First, let me plug the amazing developer who made this all possible; Jordan Eldredge!  He and several other people were able to make this an amazing and fluid experience, rendering all of the important uses Winamp people remember and loved capable, all from media drag-drop-play, to skin support, to milkdrop support. WhATTT HOW??!

You have no idea how much I’ve wanted to use this on my Mac natively, and for how long I’ve kept random installs of either Parallels or VMWare specifically just so I could use Winamp lmao 

So, if you have any of your old skins backed up (classic only, not modern from what I’ve been able to get to work), or your equalizer saves, you can still use them today in this web app! c: If you don’t have any of them any longer, you can find an amazing array uploaded to the twitter @WinampSkins, as well as on deviantART here! :D It’s as easy as drag-drop onto the app! 

It might be worth digging around any saved Geocities (Oocities) websites, or Angelfire, Tripod, etc, that withstood the tests of time to reclaim your old favorite fandom skins! I had a blast doing some deep google diving for a few!

For more information about specifics on what else it’s capable of doing besides what I played around with here, check out this Twitter status thread that shows off a bunch of mini video clips that show off features! I highly, highly recommend trying this out!
