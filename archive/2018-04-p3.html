purrincess-anxiety:

dialup2002:

I think a lot about what it was like to be a child raised by the internet in the late 90s and early 2000s. It was kind of the wild wild west in that search engines were still in their infancy and there were a lot fewer generalized hubs than there are today, so the sites you found were likely to be way different than the sites your friends found. 

You were exposed to all kinds of different stuff, more original content. While I understand you still gain exposure to different things like that now in the form of posts on social media, the charm of stumbling across someone’s hand crafted homepage or forum cannot be replicated this way.

As someone who grew up on the early internet I can confirm this. Every website had it’s own unique charm to it as well. Now alot of websites look very similar to each other.

Exactly, there’s not a lot of diversity in how websites look, even. They’re all thematic. 

Back then you could go from two web pages that would look entirely different with bright colors and exciting graphics, banners, dividers, and buttons. Obviously, people’s blogs on this website can look pretty different thanks to the themes mechanic but that’s as close as we really get. All Facebook, Twitter and Instagram profiles look exactly the same. It’s all very dull.

primordialdream says: i feel the same way, its like the internet is smaller and theres less weirdness to find in it, which im sure isnt true but i wish having cool webpages and webrings wasnt as uncommon as it is now

Yeah, exactly, I feel the same way. Technically, the internet has grown, but it feels more claustrophobic, a lot of content has been corralled on to the same websites instead of being strewn across independent websites, which on one hand is way more convenient, making it easier to find, especially for the more casual ‘net users…

But for people like us who grew up when it was less centralized, it almost makes browsing feel boring and stale because you’re less prone to finding weird sites by following links in sidebars and paging through webrings, like you mentioned. I’m happy for the convenience factor, but it’s a lot less fun to explore nowadays.
