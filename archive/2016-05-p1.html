obscuritory:

Another rare game up on the Internet Archive! Although developer MVP Software released free versions of most of the games in their back catalog, only two games in the RoboMaze trilogy were available. The first RoboMaze was apparently only released as a bonus in a commercial bundle.

As it turns out, I had a copy that was sent to me years ago, so I’ve thrown it online! RoboMaze is probably the most coherent and fun of the series, especially with the trickiness of managing your weapons and deciding when to shoot through walls. It’s actually worth playing!

It will run a bit fast in the browser emulator; press CTRL+F11 a few times to bring it down to a reasonable speed.

Jeff Brace & Dan Roganti (the artist behind all the VCF East shirts I own) set up eight networked Commodore 64′s to play a single, massive game of Space Command!  Each C64 is running Space Command! by way of a C64NIC+ cart, talking to a central server: another C64 with SuperCPU helping it along.  Amazing that these machines are all playing together.  If I were better at Missile Command (which Space Command! is modeled after) and time permitted, I would probably have sat down for a game.
